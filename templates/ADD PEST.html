Rice Pest Catalog

Explore the various pests that affect rice crops, along with descriptions and images for identification.

Green Leafhopper

The Green Leafhopper (GLH) feeds on the sap of rice plants, transmitting viruses that cause diseases such as Tungro.

Rice Bug

Rice bugs feed on the grains of rice plants, reducing the yield and damaging the quality of the rice.

Leaf Folder

Leaf folders fold leaves and feed on the tissue inside, causing damage that reduces the plant's ability to photosynthesize.

Stem Borer

Stem borers burrow into rice stems, causing the plant to wither and eventually die, leading to severe crop loss.

Zigzag Leafhopper

Zigzag leafhoppers damage rice plants by sucking the sap, and they also act as vectors for plant diseases.

Mealy Bug

Mealy bugs weaken rice plants by sucking their sap and excreting honeydew, which attracts mold and reduces crop yield.

Mole Cricket

Mole crickets damage rice plants by feeding on their roots, which leads to stunted growth and reduced yield.

Rice Hispa

Rice Hispa scrapes the surface of rice leaves, reducing the plant's ability to photosynthesize and ultimately lowering crop productivity.

Root Aphid

Root aphids damage rice plants by feeding on their roots, leading to stunted growth and poor crop development.
